## <i class="fa-solid fa-circle-info"></i>&nbsp;&nbsp;About

_Nature as a Mentor: Shinrin-yoku in Youth Work Practices_ was an Erasmus+ Training Course designed for youth workers and educators interested in integrating nature-based methods into their work with young people.

The project focused on <a href="https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Shinrin-yoku" target="_blank" rel="noopener">**Shinrin-yoku (Forest Bathing)**</a> and other holistic outdoor approaches that promote well-being, resilience, inclusion, and self-awareness. Through non-formal education, participants explored how nature can become a practical educational tool to support young people in dealing with stress, uncertainty, and the increasing pace of modern life.

## <i class="fa-solid fa-tree"></i>&nbsp;&nbsp;Activities

Throughout the week, participants took part in a variety of experiential activities, including:

- Guided forest bathing sessions
- Mindfulness and meditation practices
- Yoga and relaxation exercises
- Reflective journaling
- Outdoor team-building activities
- Workshops on youth work methodologies
- Group discussions and intercultural evenings
- Facilitation and peer-learning sessions

These activities combined personal development with practical tools that can be transferred into future youth work initiatives.

## <i class="fa-solid fa-circle-check"></i>&nbsp;&nbsp;Learning Outcomes

During this training course, I:

- Learned the principles and benefits of Shinrin-yoku as an educational practice.
- Explored how outdoor environments can foster reflection, inclusion, and emotional well-being.
- Developed practical facilitation skills for nature-based learning activities.
- Strengthened my intercultural communication and teamwork skills.
- Exchanged experiences and good practices with youth workers from across Europe.
- Reflected on the importance of preventive well-being approaches in youth work.
- Gained new tools that can be applied in future Erasmus+ projects and local youth initiatives.

## <i class="fa-solid fa-lightbulb"></i>&nbsp;&nbsp;Reflection

This training course offered much more than learning about nature, it demonstrated how slowing down, observing, and reconnecting with the environment can positively influence both personal well-being and educational practice.

Working alongside participants from different European countries created a valuable space for exchanging perspectives, sharing experiences, and discovering innovative approaches to youth work. The combination of outdoor activities, reflection, and intercultural learning made this one of the most memorable Erasmus+ experiences I've taken part in, and something i would love to keep on doing as much as possible.
